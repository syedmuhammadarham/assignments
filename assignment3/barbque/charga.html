Chicken Charga

Charagh is a deep-fried chicken dish from Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. The term Charagh is a Pashto (of the Pashtun people) language term meaning "chicken". The dish is widely popular throughout Pakistan. The whole chicken is marinated overnight in the refrigerator with sauce made of spices mixed with yoghurt, with horizontal cuts through the chicken in order to infuse the flavors. The marinated chicken is first steamed until tender and then fried in oil to gives crisp texture. The dish is typically served with naan or roti bread, and a side of salad or raita.
